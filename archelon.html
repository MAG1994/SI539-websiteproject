Archelon

One cannot talk about Mesozoic marine life without talking about Archelon. Living at the end of the Cretaceous, Archelon was the largest sea turtle ever to exist, measuring more than four meters long. Much like a modern-day Leatherback Turtle, Archelon did not have a solid shell, which probably would probably have made it too heavy for it to stay buoyant underwater. Instead, it had a set of bony struts which created an intricate skeletal framework, which, in turn, was covered by a tough, leathery exterior. The turtle's beak-like mouth had a prominent overbite, would have enabled it to cut soft-bodied animals like jellyfish into smaller pieces that it could swallow more easily.

Much like modern turtles, Archelon held a very long life span. There is one specimen in Vienna that is believed to be over a century old and probably may have longer if it hadn't died on the ocean floor. Because of the huge predators that lived in the Cretaceous oceans, like mosasaurs and sharks, Archelon may have grown to such a large size out of necessity. The huge shell may have simply been too big to fit in the mouths of most of these carnivores.
