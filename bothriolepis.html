Bothriolepis

With over 100 species that have been found on every continent, Bothriolepis was the most prosperous of all the placoderm genera. While most of them lived in fresh-water habitats, there is evidence to suggest that some species of Bothriolepis lived in the oceans, many of which inhabited the coral reefs of the Devonian Period, which helps explain how widespread they were. Bothriolepis was a very common fish in Devonian waters. Because of its fragile mouth construction, many scientists believe that it fed on algae and other small organisms. The jointed fins may have provided enough lift for the fish to get off the ocean floor and swim freely in the ocean. Some South American catfish also have armored-plating, like Bothriolepis, and also feed on bottom-dwelling algae and microorganisms. However, this resemblance is most likely just a result of similar way of life and environment; Bothriolepis has no existing relatives.
