Mixosaurus

Mixosaurus may be one of the smallest ichthyosaurs, but it's still a species worth discussing in regards to the evolution of ichthyosaurs. Earlier ichthyosaurs were eel-like in body form and lacked a dorsal fin. More modern ichthyosaurs have a dorsal fin and bear a dolphin-like appearance. Mixosaurus, meaning "mixed lizard", is regarded as a transitional species because it has some features belong to more primitive ichthyosaurs, such as its long slender tail and five-"toed" bones in its flippers, but also some features belonging to more advanced ichthyosaurs, like its present dorsal fin and longer front flippers resulting from a more active life under the water. The front flippers act like the diving planes on a submarine, steering the animal at the desired depth it wishes to travel in as it swims. In the way that modern fast-swimming fish and cetaceans have large pectoral fins in proportion to their bodies, Mixosaurus could have also used its new evolutionary traits to pursue its prey. Considering the abundance of Mixosaurus fossils from all across the globe, this body form has been highly successful.
